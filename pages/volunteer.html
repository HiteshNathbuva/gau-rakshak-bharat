Frequently Asked

Everything you want to know before joining

The most common questions from aspiring volunteers — answered clearly and honestly.

01 Who can become a volunteer with GauRakshak Bharat?

Any citizen of Bharat aged 16 and above with genuine intent to serve Gau Mata is welcome. We warmly invite students, homemakers, professionals, farmers, retired citizens and scholars — every hand and every skill adds strength to the movement.

02 How much time do I need to commit weekly?

As little as 2 hours a week is meaningful. Digital and content roles can be done remotely in your own time. Field roles typically require 4–8 hours per week or a weekend day. You choose the pace that fits your life.

03 Do I need prior experience or specific qualifications?

Not at all. Sincerity and compassion are our only prerequisites. Every new volunteer goes through role-based training, safety briefing and mentor pairing before starting active seva.

04 Is volunteering paid, or is it fully voluntary?

All roles are voluntary — offered as nishkama seva. However, on-ground field volunteers receive travel reimbursement for rescue missions, and full-time coordinators are eligible for a modest stipend after 6 months.

05 I live abroad — can I still contribute?

Absolutely. Our Digital Volunteer and Content Creator roles are open globally. NRIs and friends of Bharat contribute to translations, design, tech, storytelling and international awareness campaigns from over 15 countries.

06 Will I be given proper training and safety guidance?

Yes. Every volunteer completes a structured orientation, role-specific training and a safety & ethics module. Field volunteers get additional lawful-action briefings and are always paired with an experienced mentor.

07 Can I switch or add roles later on?

Of course. Many volunteers start with digital or event roles and later transition to field work — or take up leadership positions like district coordinator or trainer. Your journey is yours to shape.

08 What happens right after I register?

Within 48 hours, our district coordinator will reach out via WhatsApp or email with a short orientation call, next steps and your training schedule. You'll also be added to our volunteer community group for updates and support.
