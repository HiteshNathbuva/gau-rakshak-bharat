About Us

Rooted in Dharma, driven by service.

GauRakshak Bharat is a nationwide platform where citizens, scholars, farmers and volunteers unite to protect the cow — Gau Mata — and revive her role in India's agriculture, spirituality and daily life. We work through education, community outreach and lawful on-ground support.

The Sacred Bond

Why Gau Mata Matters

From the Vedas to modern research, the cow's role in shaping Bharat spans five essential dimensions.

Culture & Spirituality

Revered in Vedas, Puranas and daily rituals, Gau Mata is the living embodiment of dharma, motherhood and prosperity across Sanatan tradition.

Agriculture

Indigenous breeds fertilise soil, power natural farming and support bullock-driven agriculture — the backbone of sustainable rural India.

Environment

Cow dung and urine enrich soil, reduce chemical dependency and lower carbon footprint — restoring ecological balance to our farmlands.

Rural Economy

Dairy, panchagavya products and organic inputs generate livelihood for millions — anchoring self-reliant, village-based economies.

Health & Wellness

A2 milk, ghee and Ayurvedic formulations from desi cows nourish the body, sharpen the mind and preserve time-tested wellness traditions.

National Identity

The cow symbolises the soul of Bharat — a living link between our past, present and a compassionate, dharma-guided future.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the questions we hear most often from citizens and volunteers.

What exactly does GauRakshak Bharat do?
We are a citizen-led organisation working on cow protection, gaushala support, awareness and education. Our activities include lawful rescue coordination, natural-farming training, community satsangs, and providing infrastructure and medical aid to shelters across Bharat.
How can I become a volunteer?
Visit our Volunteer page and complete the short application form. Our district coordinators will reach out within 3 working days with an onboarding call, a code-of-conduct briefing and role options that match your availability and skills.
How do I report a cow in distress?
Use the "Report Concern" button in the navigation to submit details, location and photos. Our 24/7 helpline routes the case to the nearest volunteer team who coordinate with local authorities and veterinary services.
Are donations tax-deductible?
Yes. Contributions to registered gaushala partners are eligible for 80G tax exemption under the Income Tax Act. You will receive an official receipt on your registered email within 48 hours of your donation.
Is your work lawful and non-violent?
Absolutely. Our code of conduct is built on ahimsa and constitutional dharma. All rescues are conducted with state authorities and licensed veterinarians. Volunteers who violate this principle are removed immediately.
Can I contribute without physically volunteering?
Yes — you can help through donations, spreading awareness on social media, translating our content into regional languages, or offering professional skills (design, legal, medical, tech) remotely through our Skill Volunteer program.
